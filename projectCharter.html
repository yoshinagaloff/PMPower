Project Charter

What is a Project Charter?
A formal document that authorizes the existence of a project.
Grants the Project Manager authority to apply organizational resources.
Created and approved by the Project Sponsor (PM may draft but cannot approve).
Why is the Project Charter needed?
  • Provides official approval and legitimizes the project.
  • Ensures alignment with the organization’s strategic objectives.
  • Serves as the single source of truth for scope, objectives, and boundaries.
  • Prevents scope creep by defining high-level scope from the start.
  • Shows organizational commitment and provides a foundation for planning.
  • Improves communication between Sponsor, PM, and stakeholders.
Typical Contents of a Project Charter
  • Project purpose / business justification
  • Measurable objectives & success criteria
  • High-level scope and deliverables
  • High-level requirements
  • Major risks, assumptions, constraints
  • Summary milestone schedule
  • Summary budget / approved financial resources
  • Key stakeholders
  • Assigned Project Manager + authority level
  • Approval requirements
Who creates and updates the Project Charter?
  • Created by: Project Sponsor (or someone authorized by Sponsor)
  • Approved by: Sponsor
  • PM’s role: May draft/support writing, but cannot approve
  • Updates: Only Sponsor (PM may propose changes)
