Perform Integrated Change Control - Interactive Steps

Record all change requests in the change management system
Scenario & Analysis:

You are the project manager of a complex infrastructure project. Late in the project, a key stakeholder submits a formal change request to add new features that could improve user satisfaction but will potentially delay the project schedule and increase costs.

What is your first step in handling this change request?

  • A. Immediately approve and implement the change to satisfy the stakeholder.
  • B. Document the change request officially in the change management system for evaluation.
  • C. Reject the change request to avoid schedule and cost risks.
  • D. Inform the project team about the requested change and ask for opinions.
Correct Answer: B. Document the change request officially in the change management system for evaluation.
Explanation: Even if the change might be beneficial, the first formal step is to record it properly to ensure traceability and controlled evaluation before any action.
Evaluate impact on cost, schedule, and scope before approval
Scenario & Analysis:

After documenting the change request, your project team is divided. Some believe the new feature adds value, others worry about budget overruns and schedule slippage.

What critical factors should you evaluate before deciding on this change request?

  • A. Team members' opinions and preferences
  • B. Customer satisfaction only
  • C. Impact on project cost, schedule, and scope baselines
  • D. Quality assurance processes
Correct Answer: C. Impact on project cost, schedule, and scope baselines
Explanation: Before approval, analyze how the change affects key project baselines to maintain control over project objectives and avoid negative surprises.
Approve, defer, or reject changes through formal review
Scenario & Analysis:

Following the CCB meeting, the board has reviewed all inputs, including cost estimates and risk analysis.

What options do you have regarding the change request outcome?

  • A. Approve, defer, or reject the change request
  • B. Immediately implement the change without further approval
  • C. Ignore the request and continue with the original plan
  • D. Ask the customer to withdraw the request
Correct Answer: A. Approve, defer, or reject the change request
Explanation: The formal review process allows the CCB to decide to approve, defer for more information, or reject the change based on comprehensive evaluation.
Use the Change Control Board (CCB) when applicable to review and decide on changes
Scenario & Analysis:

The change request impacts multiple project areas and involves budget increases. Your organization has a Change Control Board (CCB).

Who should be involved in the evaluation and approval of this change request?

  • A. Project sponsor only
  • B. Project manager alone
  • C. The Change Control Board (CCB)
  • D. Quality assurance team
Correct Answer: C. The Change Control Board (CCB)
Explanation: The CCB is a formally authorized group responsible for reviewing and deciding on important changes that impact the project significantly.
Update project plan and documents accordingly
Scenario & Analysis:

Once the change request is approved, what should be your next action regarding project documentation?

  • A. Ignore updates and proceed
  • B. Update the project management plan, schedules, and related documents
  • C. Notify the team verbally only
  • D. Cancel the project baseline
Correct Answer: B. Update the project management plan, schedules, and related documents
Explanation: Approved changes must be formally reflected in all relevant project documents to maintain alignment and traceability.
Communicate approved changes to stakeholders
Scenario & Analysis:

After updating the project plan, how should you ensure all stakeholders are informed of the approved changes?

  • A. Send official notifications through established communication channels
  • B. Only inform the core project team
  • C. Delay communication until project completion
  • D. Assume stakeholders will find out themselves
Correct Answer: A. Send official notifications through established communication channels
Explanation: Effective communication ensures transparency and alignment among all stakeholders, reducing risks of miscoordination.
